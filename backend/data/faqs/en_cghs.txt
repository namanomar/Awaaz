Q: What is the Central Government Health Scheme (CGHS)?
A: The Central Government Health Scheme, known as CGHS, provides comprehensive medical care. It covers central government employees, pensioners, and their eligible family members.

Q: Who is eligible for the CGHS scheme?
A: Serving central government employees, retired pensioners drawing pension from central civil estimates, and widows of central government employees are eligible. Employees of autonomous bodies may also be covered if the scheme is extended to them.

Q: How do I apply for a CGHS card?
A: If you are a serving employee, you should apply for your CGHS card through your department or office. Pensioners need to apply at the CGHS office located in their city.

Q: What is the monthly contribution for CGHS?
A: The monthly contribution for CGHS varies based on your pay band or pension level. It ranges from Rs 250 per month to Rs 1,000 per month.

Q: What services are available at CGHS Wellness Centres?
A: CGHS Wellness Centres offer OPD consultations, provide medicines, and facilitate diagnostic tests. There are over 330 such centres located in 79 cities across India.

Q: How can I get specialist or hospital treatment under CGHS?
A: To receive specialist or hospital treatment, you must first obtain a referral. This referral needs to be issued by a CGHS Medical Officer.

Q: Can I get treatment at private hospitals with CGHS?
A: Yes, you can receive cashless treatment at private hospitals that are empanelled with CGHS. A list of these hospitals is available on the official website, cghs.gov.in. For any queries, you can call the helpline at 1800-11-4477.
