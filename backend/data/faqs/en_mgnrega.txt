Q: What is MGNREGA?
A: MGNREGA (Mahatma Gandhi National Rural Employment Guarantee Act) guarantees 100 days of wage employment per year to every rural household whose adult members volunteer to do unskilled manual work. The current wage rate varies by state, typically between Rs 200–350 per day. Call helpline 1800-111-555 to know your state's wage rate.

Q: Who is eligible for MGNREGA?
A: Any adult member of a rural household who is willing to do unskilled manual work is eligible. You must be a resident of a rural area and registered with your local gram panchayat. There is no income limit — MGNREGA is open to all rural households.

Q: How do I apply for a job card under MGNREGA?
A: Visit your local gram panchayat office and submit an application with your household details, name of adult members, and your address. The gram panchayat must issue a job card within 15 days of application. The job card is free of cost.

Q: What documents are needed for MGNREGA registration?
A: You need proof of identity (Aadhaar card), proof of residence, and a recent passport-size photograph for each adult member. A ration card or voter ID can also serve as proof of residence.

Q: How do I demand work under MGNREGA?
A: Submit a written application to your gram panchayat demanding work. The panchayat must provide work within 15 days of your demand. If work is not provided within 15 days, you are entitled to an unemployment allowance.

Q: What is the unemployment allowance under MGNREGA?
A: If work is not provided within 15 days of demand, you get an unemployment allowance of at least one-fourth of the wage rate for the first 30 days, and one-half of the wage rate for the remaining period in a financial year.

Q: How is payment made under MGNREGA?
A: Wages are paid directly to your bank account or post office account within 15 days of completing the work. Make sure your bank account is linked to your job card. Contact your gram panchayat if payment is delayed.
