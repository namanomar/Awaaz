Q: What is NSAP — National Social Assistance Programme?
A: NSAP (National Social Assistance Programme) is a central government welfare scheme providing financial assistance to elderly people, widows, and persons with disabilities who are BPL (Below Poverty Line). It includes Indira Gandhi National Old Age Pension, Widow Pension, and Disability Pension. Apply at your district social welfare office.

Q: Who is eligible for the old age pension under NSAP?
A: Men and women aged 60 years and above who belong to BPL households are eligible. Those aged 60–79 receive Rs 200 per month from the central government (states may add more). Those aged 80 and above receive Rs 500 per month.

Q: Who is eligible for the widow pension under NSAP?
A: Widows aged 40–79 years who belong to BPL households are eligible for the Indira Gandhi National Widow Pension Scheme, with a central contribution of Rs 300 per month. States often top this up.

Q: Who is eligible for the disability pension under NSAP?
A: Persons aged 18–79 with 80% or more disability who belong to BPL households are eligible for the Indira Gandhi National Disability Pension of Rs 300 per month from the centre.

Q: How do I apply for NSAP pension?
A: Visit your local gram panchayat (rural areas) or municipality/ward office (urban areas) or the district social welfare office. Submit the application form with required documents. Your block development officer can also help.

Q: What documents are needed for NSAP pension?
A: You need Aadhaar card, age proof (birth certificate or school certificate), income/BPL certificate from tehsildar or village officer, bank account details, and passport-size photograph. For widow pension, death certificate of husband. For disability pension, disability certificate from a government hospital.

Q: How long does NSAP pension take to start?
A: After submitting the application, it is typically processed within 60–90 days. Pension is deposited directly to your bank account. Follow up with your gram panchayat or social welfare office if it takes longer.
