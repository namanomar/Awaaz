Q: Who is eligible for the Ayushman Bharat PMJAY scheme?
A: Eligibility for Ayushman Bharat PMJAY is primarily based on the Socio-Economic Caste Census (SECC) 2011 database. Families listed in this database are eligible. For rural families, there is no income cut-off, while urban families must meet 11 specific occupational criteria.

Q: How much health coverage does Ayushman Bharat PMJAY provide?
A: Ayushman Bharat PMJAY provides health coverage of Rs 5 lakh per year for each eligible family. This coverage is for secondary and tertiary care hospitalization. It aims to support over 10 crore poor and vulnerable families across India.

Q: What types of treatments are covered under Ayushman Bharat PMJAY?
A: The scheme covers over 1,949 treatment packages, which include surgery, medical treatments, and day care procedures. It also covers both pre-hospitalisation and post-hospitalisation costs. Importantly, pre-existing conditions are covered from day one of enrollment.

Q: How can I get my Ayushman Bharat PMJAY card?
A: To get your Ayushman Bharat PMJAY card, you should visit your nearest Common Service Centre or any empanelled hospital. You will need to bring your Aadhaar card and ration card. The card itself is provided free of charge to beneficiaries.

Q: What documents are required to avail benefits under Ayushman Bharat PMJAY?
A: You will primarily need your Aadhaar card to get your PMJAY card. For rural families, a ration card is also required. A Voter ID can be used as an alternative document if you don't have a ration card.

Q: Do I need to pay any premium or registration fee for Ayushman Bharat PMJAY?
A: No, beneficiaries are not required to pay any premium or registration fee for the Ayushman Bharat PMJAY scheme. It is a fully government-funded scheme designed to provide free health coverage to eligible poor and vulnerable families.

Q: Which hospitals accept Ayushman Bharat PMJAY and is there a helpline for more information?
A: Both government and private hospitals across India are empanelled under Ayushman Bharat PMJAY, with over 25,000 hospitals participating. You can check the list of empanelled hospitals on pmjay.gov.in/empanelledHospitals. For any queries or assistance, you can call the toll-free helpline number 14555, which is available 24x7.
